How Does Depression Physically Affect the Brain?

By: Renee Onque

March 23, 2021

There have been constant debates about if and how depression affects the human brain. The common misconception is that depression only affects people mentally, but there are also physical effects that depression has on the most important organ of our bodies. Click below to discover how depression affects different sections of the brain.

Amygdala

Depression causes the amygdala to become overly active which sends messages to your body to produce sad stimuli that can appear in the form of sad faces. Stress hormones are also released by the amygdala when a person is experiencing depression.

Hippocampus

This part of the brain shrinks in people who have constant struggles with depression or people who haven't treated their depression. The hippocampus is responsible for storing memories and emotion, so when this shrinks, a person's capability of remembering things decreases.

Dorsomedial Thalamus

Some studies show that the thamulus increases activation by producing a larger number of nerve cells when people are depressed. People with major depressive disorders also tend to have a larger thalamus than others. The thalamus is responsible for thinking, talking, seeing and moving.
